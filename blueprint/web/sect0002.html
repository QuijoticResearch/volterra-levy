The Volterra-Lévy Itô decomposition, the \(\nu \)-singular/\(\nu \)-regular splitting, the defect vanishing characterization, and \(\nu \)-singularity persistence.

2.1 Process, Payoff, and Bridge

Definition 11 Volterra process data

CombinedEnergySpace.VolterraProcessData def:CombinedEnergySpace Data for a Volterra process: \(V_T = \delta _W(\text{kernel\_ cont}) + \tilde{\delta }_J(\text{kernel\_ jump})\).

Definition 12 Smooth payoff

CombinedEnergySpace.SmoothPayoff def:CombinedEnergySpace A smooth function \(f\) applied to \(V_T\). Carries \(f\), \(f'\), the Taylor remainder \(g_f^K(v,z) = f(v+Kz) - f(v) - f'(v)Kz\), with \(g_f^K(v,0) = 0\) and a uniform quadratic bound \(|g_f^K(v,z)| \leq C_{\mathrm{defect}} \cdot z^2\).

Definition 13 Hilbert-jump bridge

CombinedEnergySpace.HilbertJumpBridge def:VolterraProcessData, def:SmoothPayoff Connects the Hilbert Itô formula (OperatorDerivative.lean) and the Banach defect theory (BanachLevyComplete.lean) on \(H_W \times \tilde{H}_J\). Carries the ONE stochastic analysis input (levy_ito: the Lévy-Itô formula from Applebaum [2], Theorem 4.4.7), the Taylor split, the defect coherence, and the payoff-process connection.

2.3 Moment Structure and Singularity

Definition 19 \(\nu \)-moment structure

NuMomentStructure Captures the integrability dichotomy for stable-type measures with index \(\gamma \in (1,2)\): \(\int |z|^p\, \nu (dz) = \infty \) for \(p {\lt} \gamma \), \(\int |z|^{2p}\, \nu (dz) {\lt} \infty \) for \(2p {\gt} \gamma \).

Definition 24 \(\nu \)-singularity data

NuSingularityData The kernel norm \(\int _0^T |K(T,s)|^p\, ds {\gt} 0\) and truncated \(z\)-moments \(\int _{|z| \leq R} |z|^p\, \nu (dz)\) which are unbounded as \(R \to \infty \).

Theorem 25 \(\nu \)-singularity persists (Proposition 6.x)

CombinedEnergySpace.nu_singularity_persists def:NuMomentStructure, def:NuSingularityData \(\forall M,\; \exists R,\; \| K\| _p \cdot \int _{|z| \leq R} |z|^p\, \nu (dz) {\gt} M\). The product norm of \(K(T,\cdot )z\) exceeds any bound, hence \(V_T \notin \mathrm{im}(\tilde{\delta }_J)\). CONCRETE: from mul_lt_mul_of_pos_left and field_simp.

Proof â–¼

Divide \(M\) by kernel_p_norm, use truncated_z_moment_unbounded, multiply back.
