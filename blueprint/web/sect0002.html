2 The Kernel-Weighted Defect

The Volterra–Lévy Itô decomposition, the \(\nu \)-singular/\(\nu \)-regular splitting, the defect vanishing characterization, and \(\nu \)-singularity persistence. The central object is the kernel-weighted Leibniz defect \(\Gamma _V^f = \tilde{\delta }_J(g_f^K)\), which equals the structurally unhedgeable component of risk [1, Theorem 5.2].

2.1 Process, Payoff, and Bridge

Definition 11 Volterra process data

CombinedEnergySpace.VolterraProcessData def:CombinedEnergySpace Data for a Volterra process: \(V_T = \delta _W(\text{kernel\_ cont}) + \tilde{\delta }_J(\text{kernel\_ jump})\), decomposing the terminal value into its continuous and jump stochastic integrals against the kernel \(K(T,\cdot )\) [1, Definition 2.1, eq. (2.2)].

Definition 12 Smooth payoff

CombinedEnergySpace.SmoothPayoff def:CombinedEnergySpace A smooth function \(f \in C_b^3(\mathbb {R})\) applied to \(V_T\). Carries \(f\), \(f'\), the Taylor remainder \(g_f^K(v,z) = f(v+Kz) - f(v) - f'(v)Kz\) (the kernel-weighted defect integrand), with \(g_f^K(v,0) = 0\) and a uniform quadratic bound \(|g_f^K(v,z)| \leq C_{\mathrm{defect}} \cdot z^2\) where \(C_{\mathrm{defect}} = \tfrac {1}{2}\| f''\| _\infty K(T,t)^2\) [1, Theorem 3.1, eq. (3.7)].

Definition 13 Hilbert-jump bridge

CombinedEnergySpace.HilbertJumpBridge def:VolterraProcessData, def:SmoothPayoff Connects the Hilbert Itô formula (from OperatorDerivative.lean [3]) and the Banach defect theory (from BanachLevyComplete.lean [4]) on the combined space \(H_W \times \tilde{H}_J\). The bridge carries exactly one stochastic analysis input: the Lévy–Itô formula (levy_ito, from Applebaum [2], Theorem 4.4.7) applied to the semimartingale \(V_T(t) = \int _0^t K(T,s)\, dL_s\). It also carries the Taylor split (algebraic), the defect coherence (connecting the abstract Banach element to the pointwise formula), and the payoff-process connection. The dependency chain is: process \(\to \) payoff \(\to \) bridge \(\to \) decomposition theorem.

2.2 The Volterra–Lévy Itô Decomposition

Definition 14 Kernel-weighted defect

CombinedEnergySpace.kernel_weighted_defect def:HilbertJumpBridge \(\Gamma _V^f := \tilde{\delta }_J(g_f^K)\), the compensated Poisson integral of the defect integrand. This is the kernel-weighted Leibniz defect—the precise content invisible to Malliavin calculus, and the structurally unhedgeable component of risk in Volterra–Lévy markets [1, Theorem 5.2, eq. (5.10)].

2.3 Moment Structure and Singularity

Definition 19 \(\nu \)-moment structure

NuMomentStructure Captures the integrability dichotomy for stable-type Lévy measures with index \(\gamma \in (1,2)\) [1, Assumption L1]: \(\int |z|^p\, \nu (dz) = \infty \) for \(p {\lt} \gamma \) (\(\nu \)-singular regime) and \(\int |z|^{2p}\, \nu (dz) {\lt} \infty \) for \(2p {\gt} \gamma \) (\(\nu \)-regular regime). The working exponent \(p \in (1,\gamma )\) and the small-jump constant \(c_\gamma \) from the stable density bound \(\nu (dz) \sim c_\gamma |z|^{-(1+\gamma )}\, dz\).

Definition 24 \(\nu \)-singularity data

NuSingularityData The kernel norm \(\int _0^T |K(T,s)|^p\, ds {\gt} 0\) (finite by the kernel regularity assumption [1, Assumption K1]) and truncated \(z\)-moments \(\int _{|z| \leq R} |z|^p\, \nu (dz)\) which are unbounded as \(R \to \infty \) (since \(p {\lt} \gamma \) and \(\nu \) is \(\gamma \)-stable near zero).

Theorem 25 \(\nu \)-singularity persists (Proposition 6.x)

CombinedEnergySpace.nu_singularity_persists def:NuMomentStructure, def:NuSingularityData \(\forall M,\; \exists R,\; \| K\| _p \cdot \int _{|z| \leq R} |z|^p\, \nu (dz) {\gt} M\). The \(\tilde{H}_J\)-norm of the linear integrand \(K(T,\cdot )z\) is infinite: the product of a positive kernel norm and an unbounded \(z\)-moment exceeds any bound. Hence \(V_T \notin \mathrm{im}(\tilde{\delta }_J)\): the kernel cannot regularize the jump-size singularity, because \(K\) acts on time while the singularity is in \(z\) [1, Proposition 6.1]. CONCRETE: from mul_lt_mul_of_pos_left and field_simp.

Proof â–¼

Divide \(M\) by kernel_p_norm, use truncated_z_moment_unbounded, multiply back.

2.4 Defect Vanishing

Definition 26 Jump presence

CombinedEnergySpace.JumpPresence def:HilbertJumpBridge Whether the Lévy measure is nontrivial. When \(\nu = 0\) (no jumps), all jump sizes are \(z = 0\), so by defect_at_zero (the Taylor remainder at zero displacement is zero), the defect integrand vanishes in \(\tilde{H}_J\). This connects to the fundamental dichotomy: \(D\) satisfies the Leibniz rule if and only if the driver has no jumps [4, Theorem B].

Theorem 27 Defect vanishes without jumps (Proposition 7.x)

CombinedEnergySpace.defect_vanishes_without_jumps_volterra def:JumpPresence, def:kernel_weighted_defect When \(\nu = 0\): \(\Gamma _V^f = 0\). The kernel-weighted defect vanishes, recovering perfect hedging in pure rough volatility models (El Euch–Rosenbaum 2019). The defect IS the non-Gaussian content: it vanishes precisely when the driver is Gaussian [1, Proposition 7.1]. ALGEBRAIC: from no_jumps_zero_defect and map_zero.

Proof â–¼

Rewrite with zero defect integrand, apply map_zero.
